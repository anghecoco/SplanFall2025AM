Boletus rex-veris: The Spring King

Introduction

Boletus rex-veris, commonly known as the spring king bolete, is a basidiomycete fungus of the genus Boletus found in western North America. The large, edible fruiting bodies appear under pine trees, generally in May to June. It has a pinkish to brownish cap and a thick, often swollen stem; overall color may show an orange-red tinge. As with other boletes, the size of the fruiting body is variable. B. rex-veris is edible and may be preserved and cooked.

Taxonomy

For many years, Boletus rex-veris was considered a subspecies or form of the porcini mushroom B. edulis. In 2008, a taxonomic revision of western North American populations was published, formally establishing it as a distinct species, Boletus rex-veris. Phylogenetic analysis places B. rex-veris in a clade with B. fibrillosus, B. pinophilus, B. subcaerulescens, B. subalpinus, and B. regineus.

People and Fungi

In culinary contexts, the Spring King is appreciated for its firm texture and pleasant, nutty aroma when cooked.
