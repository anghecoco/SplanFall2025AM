FEATURED: Boletus edulis var. edulis

Overview

Boletus edulis, known as the porcini or king bolete, is a highly regarded edible mushroom native to the Northern Hemisphere. It is celebrated for its thick stem, brown cap, and nutty flavor, and is a staple in European cuisine.

The species forms symbiotic relationships with trees in forests and is widely collected in the wild. Porcini mushrooms are especially valued in Italian cooking, often featured in risottos, pasta dishes, and soups.
